Software licenses and the open source software movement are interconnected with the issue of software piracy, which is a significant concern in the digital world. While open source software encourages collaboration and sharing, piracy arises due to various reasons. One of the main causes is the high cost of proprietary software, which makes it inaccessible to many individuals, particularly in developing countries or underprivileged communities. The desire to access expensive software without paying leads some individuals to resort to piracy. Additionally, limited awareness and understanding of intellectual property rights, coupled with the ease of online sharing, contribute to the problem. The availability of cracked or pirated software through illegal channels further fuels piracy.
